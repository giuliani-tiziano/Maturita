Employment in Tech

IT Roles · CV & Cover Letter · Technical Interviews · Remote Work

Overview

Working in the tech industry

The technology sector is one of the fastest-growing employment sectors worldwide. IT professionals work in virtually every industry — finance, healthcare, entertainment, government, manufacturing. Understanding IT job roles, how to apply for positions, and how to perform in technical interviews (in English) is an essential skill for any IT graduate.

Application

CV and Cover Letter in English

CV / Résumé — key sections

  1. Personal information — name, email, LinkedIn, GitHub.
  2. Professional summary — 2–3 lines: who you are, what you offer.
  3. Technical skills — languages, frameworks, tools (Python, Java, SQL, Docker…).
  4. Work experience — bullet points starting with action verbs: "Developed", "Implemented", "Reduced"…
  5. Education — degree, institution, graduation year.
  6. Projects — personal or academic projects with GitHub links.

Cover letter structure

A cover letter (max one page) explains why you want this job and why you are the right candidate:

  1. Opening — state the position and where you saw it advertised.
  2. Body paragraph 1 — your most relevant experience/skills.
  3. Body paragraph 2 — why this company specifically (show you researched them).
  4. Closing — express enthusiasm; request an interview; thank them.

Key phrases: "I am writing to apply for…", "My experience in X has equipped me with…", "I would welcome the opportunity to…"

Interviews

Technical interviews

Types of technical interview questions

  • Coding challenges — solve an algorithmic problem (often live, using a whiteboard or online editor). Common platforms: LeetCode, HackerRank.
  • System design — "Design a URL shortener." Tests architecture thinking at scale.
  • Behavioural — "Tell me about a time you solved a difficult bug." Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
  • Knowledge questions — "What is the difference between TCP and UDP?" "Explain REST."

Remote work and global teams

Much of the tech industry operates remotely or in globally distributed teams. English is the lingua franca of international tech teams. Key skills:

  • Asynchronous communication — clear written communication (Slack, email, Jira tickets).
  • Documentation — writing clear technical documentation that colleagues across time zones can understand.
  • Stand-ups — daily team meetings (often in English): "Yesterday I worked on… Today I will… I'm blocked by…"
