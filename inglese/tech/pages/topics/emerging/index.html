Topic 20 · Emerging Technologies

Emerging Technologies

Blockchain · VR/AR · Quantum Computing · 5G · Automation & Jobs

Overview

The technologies reshaping the future

Emerging technologies are innovations that are currently developing or will be developed over the next decade, and that are expected to significantly alter the social, economic and technological landscape. For IT professionals, understanding these trends is essential for career planning and for engaging in the public debates they generate.

Blockchain

Blockchain & distributed ledgers

What is blockchain?

A blockchain is a distributed, immutable ledger — a database replicated across thousands of computers (nodes) with no central authority. Data is stored in blocks, each containing a cryptographic hash of the previous block, creating an unbreakable chain. Once data is recorded, it cannot be altered without changing all subsequent blocks — making fraud computationally infeasible.

Applications: cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum), supply chain tracking, smart contracts, digital identity, voting systems.

Smart contracts

A smart contract is self-executing code stored on a blockchain. When predefined conditions are met, the contract executes automatically without any intermediary (bank, lawyer, notary).

Example: a smart contract for a house purchase automatically transfers ownership and payment when all conditions (inspection passed, funds available) are verified.

Ethereum is the leading platform for smart contracts.

VR & AR

Virtual and Augmented Reality

VR — Virtual Reality

VR creates a fully immersive digital environment, replacing the real world. Users wear a headset (Meta Quest, PlayStation VR) that tracks head movement and renders a 360° virtual world. Applications: gaming, training simulations (surgery, military, pilots), virtual tourism, remote collaboration ("metaverse").

AR — Augmented Reality

AR overlays digital content onto the real world — the physical environment remains visible but is enhanced with digital information. Examples: Pokémon GO, IKEA Place (see furniture in your room), surgical AR (showing vital signs overlaid on the patient), industrial maintenance guides.

Mixed Reality (MR) = advanced AR where digital objects interact with real objects (Microsoft HoloLens).

Future

Quantum computing & 5G

Quantum computing

Classical computers use bits (0 or 1). Quantum computers use qubits, which can be 0, 1, or both simultaneously (superposition) and can be correlated with each other (entanglement). This allows quantum computers to solve certain problems exponentially faster than classical computers.

Potential applications: drug discovery, materials science, optimisation, cryptography (quantum computers could break RSA encryption — driving the development of post-quantum cryptography).
