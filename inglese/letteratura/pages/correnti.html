1750–1790
Pre-Romanticism
Graveyard poets, sensibility, the origins of the Sublime. Gray's Elegy, Ossian, the beginnings of emotional poetry.

1785–1830
Romanticism
Imagination over reason, the Sublime in nature, the Lyrical Ballads. Blake, Wordsworth, Coleridge, Byron, Shelley, Keats.

Gothic Novel
Mystery, terror, the supernatural. Horace Walpole's Castle of Otranto, Mary Shelley's Frankenstein, Bram Stoker's Dracula.

The Age of Industrialisation, the serial novel, social realism. Dickens, the Brontës, Thomas Hardy and Darwin's impact.

"Art for Art's Sake" — beauty over morality, the dandy, Decadentism. Oscar Wilde and Walter Pater's influence.

Modernism
Stream of consciousness, fragmentation, interior monologue. WWI's trauma reshapes form and meaning in Woolf, Joyce and Eliot.

Dystopian Fiction
Imagined totalitarian futures as political warning — Huxley's Brave New World, Orwell's 1984, Atwood's The Handmaid's Tale.

Meaninglessness, existential paralysis, no resolution. Beckett's Waiting for Godot, Ionesco. Post-WWII philosophical nihilism on stage.
